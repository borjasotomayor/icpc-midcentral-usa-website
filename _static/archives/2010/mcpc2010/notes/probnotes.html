Notes on the Problem Set Last modified Oct 24, 2010

The Regional Chief Judges (Andy Harrington, Gabriel Foust, and Michael Goldwasser) rank the problems roughly as follows, with the author's name in parentheses:

Very Easy
Voting (Andy)
Mirror (Michael)
Easy
Judges' Time Calculation (Michael)
Moderate
Mad Scientist (Michael)
Egyptian Fractions (Gabriel)
Medium
Image Compression (Michael)
Queen Collisions (Andy)
Hard
Top This (Gabriel/Michael/Andy)
Very Hard
Quick Search (Andy)

There is a vast range of difficulties.  We expect most teams to get one or more of the easy ones.  Egyptian fractions is short to code, but does require knowing how to reduce to lowest terms efficiently.  Top This and Image Compresion require some thought and array manipulations.  Queen Collisions requires some thought about the algorithm to run fast enough.  Quick Search will hopefully provide a challenge to the best teams.  We expect a few teams to solve seven or eight problems, but very few to solve all nine.  

We need your help spreading out the work next year!  The addition of Michael Goldwasser this year was a boon.  More new blood would be even better!
